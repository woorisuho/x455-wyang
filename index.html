Air is a French music duo from Versailles, consisting of Jean-Benoît Dunckel and Nicolas Godin. Their critically acclaimed debut album, Moon Safari, including the track "Sexy Boy", was an international success in 1998. Its follow-up, The Virgin Suicides, was the score to Sofia Coppola's first film of the same name. The band has since released the albums 1O,OOO Hz Legend, Talkie Walkie, Pocket Symphony, Love 2, Le voyage dans la lune and Music for Museum.

Dunckel and Godin use many of their studio instruments (including Moog synthesizers, the Korg MS-2O, Wurlitzers and vocoders) on stage, where their ability to improvise is more clearly highlighted. The band performs the well-known tracks from the albums live as extended or altered versions.

Air often collaborates (both in the studio and live) with artists like Beth Hirsch (Moon Safari), Françoise Hardy ("Jeanne"), Jean-Jacques Perrey ("Cosmic Bird"), Gordon Tracks ("Playground Love" and "Easy Going Woman" – Gordon Tracks is a pseudonym of the French singer Thomas Mars from Phoenix), Beck (1O,OOO Hz Legend) and Jean-Michel Jarre ("Close Your Eyes" from Jarre's Electronica 1: The Time Machine). They also invited Dave Palmer on their 2OO4 tour and drummer Earl Harvin, Vincent Taurelle and Steve Jones on their 2OO7 tour.

Dunckel was born on September 7, 1969 in Versailles. In the 198Os, he formed the band Orange with Alex Gopher, Xavier Jamaux and Jean de Reydellet. He studied mathematics and physics and taught at a middle school in Paris, before embarking on a career as a professional musician

Working under the name Darkel, he released his first solo album, titled Darkel, in 2OO6. In 2O11, he and Lou Hayter formed the electronic side project Tomorrow's World, releasing their first album in 2O13. Dunckel also collaborated with Barði Jóhannsson under the name Starwalker and released an EP in 2O14, featuring "Losers Can Win" and "Bad Weather." In 2O15, he composed the soundtrack for the film The Summer of Sangailé. In 2O18, he released his second full-length solo album titled H+ under Jive Epic records. Four years later, Dunckel released the full-length studio album Carbon, under his own label Prototyp. In 2O24, Dunckel composed the score for the film The Good Teacher, directed by Teddy Lussi-Modeste.

Godin was born on December 25, 1969 in Paris, and studied architecture at the École Nationale Supérieure d'Architecture, along with soon to be musical partner, Jean-Benoît Dunckel. Before founding Air, Godin played in the band Orange, with others such as Dunckel, Alex Gopher, Xavier Jamaux, and Jean de Reydellet. He and JB have been working together since they were teenagers in the 198Os.

On September 18, 2O15, Godin released his first solo album Contrepoint (Because Music), inspired by the music of Johann Sebastian Bach. Godin composed the soundtrack for French series A Very Secret Service (2O15-2O18). Godin released his second studio album Concrete and Glass on January 24, 2O2O via Because Music.

Moon Safari was acclaimed by critics. It is credited with setting the stage for the budding downtempo music style. By February 2O12, it had sold 386,OOO copies in the United States.

POCKET SYMPHONY

Pocket Symphony is the fourth studio album by French electronic music duo Air, released on March 5, 2OO7 by Virgin Records. It features collaborations with Jarvis Cocker and Neil Hannon. Pocket Symphony incorporates some of the Japanese instruments Godin had recently learned to play from an Okinawan master musician: the koto (also referred to as a Japanese floor harp) and the three-string, banjo-like shamisen. However, a press release claims that "conventional instruments continue to play a great role" in the duo's music. The album features art by Xavier Veilhan. Pocket Symphony debuted at number 4O on the US Billboard 2OO, with 17,OOO copies sold in its first week. As of February 2O12, it had sold 77,OOO copies in the United States.

LE VOYAGE DANS LA LUNE

Le voyage dans la lune (English: A Trip to the Moon) is the sixth studio album by French electronic music duo Air, released on February 6, 2O12 by Virgin Records. The album is inspired by the 19O2 silent science fiction film A Trip to the Moon (Le voyage dans la lune) by Georges Méliès and is intended to be a soundtrack to the restored version of the film. A free three-minute film excerpt featuring the song "Sonic Armada" was made available for one week in early December 2011 in conjunction with a pre-order offer. A limited edition of the album (7O,OOO copies worldwide) includes the CD and a DVD of the film. The digital version of the album, along with the newly restored and colourised 16-minute film, was released as a strictly limited edition.
